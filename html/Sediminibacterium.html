Genus Sediminibacterium

\r\n \r\n

\r\n Warning: In the List of Prokaryotic names with Standing in Nomenclature, an arrow (→) only indicates the sequence of valid publication of names and does not mean that the last name in the sequence must be used (see: Introduction).\r\n

\r\n

\r\n Number of species, including synonyms, cited in this file: 5 \r\n
\r\n Number of subspecies, including synonyms, cited in this file: 0 \r\n

\r\n

\r\n Classification (Warning: see also the file "Classification of prokaryotes: Introduction").\r\n

\r\n

\r\n  \r\n

\r\n

\r\n Sediminibacterium Qu and Yuan 2008, gen. nov.\r\n
\r\n Type species: ¤ Sediminibacterium salmoneum Qu and Yuan 2008.\r\n
\r\n Etymology: L. n. sedimen -inis, sediment; L. neut. n. bacterium, a rod; N.L. neut. n. Sediminibacterium, a rod from sediment.\r\n
\r\n Valid publication: QU (J.H.) and YUAN (H.L.): Sediminibacterium salmoneum gen. nov., sp. nov., a member of the phylum Bacteroidetes isolated from sediment of a eutrophic reservoir. Int. J. Syst. Evol. Microbiol., 2008, 58, 2191-2194.\r\n
\r\n Original article in IJSEM Online\r\n

\r\n

→ Sediminibacterium Qu and Yuan 2008 emend. Kim et al. 2013.\r\n
\r\n Reference: KIM (Y.J.), NGUYEN (N.L.), WEON (H.Y.) and YANG (D.C.): Sediminibacterium ginsengisoli sp. nov., isolated from soil of a ginseng field, and emended descriptions of the genus Sediminibacterium and of Sediminibacterium salmoneum. Int. J. Syst. Evol. Microbiol., 2013, 63, 905-912.\r\n
\r\n Original article in IJSEM Online\r\n

Whole-genome sequence accession no. for the type strain: . \r\n \r\n

Source: Environmental - freshwater. \r\n \r\n
Valid publication: KIM, Y., KIM, B., KANG, K. and AHN, T. Y. 2016. Sediminibacterium aquarii sp. nov., isolated from sediment in a fishbowl. Int. J. Syst. Evol. Microbiol., 66, 4501-4505. \r\n \r\n

Sediminibacterium goheungense Kang et al. 2014, sp. nov.\r\n\r\n
Type strain: (see also Global Catalogue of Microorganisms) HME7863=KCTC 23945=CECT 8100.\r\n\r\n
Sequence accession no. (16S rRNA gene) for the type strain: JN674641.\r\n\r\n
Etymology: N.L. neut. adj goheungense, pertaining to Goheung in the Republic of Korea, where the type strain was isolated.\r\n\r\n
Source: Environmental - freshwater. \r\n \r\n
Valid publication: KANG, H., KIM, H., LEE, B.-I., JOUNG, Y. and JOH, K. 2014. Sediminibacterium goheungense sp. nov., isolated from a freshwater reservoir. Int. J. Syst. Evol. Microbiol., 64, 1328-1333. \r\n \r\n
